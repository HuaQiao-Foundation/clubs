# Timeline User Guide

**Georgetown Rotary Club - Annual Timeline System**
**Last Updated**: 2025-10-13
**For**: Club Officers, Board Members, and Chairs

---

## Overview

The Timeline System provides a comprehensive view of Georgetown Rotary's annual history, displaying leadership themes, club statistics, service projects, and speakers for each Rotary year (July 1 - June 30).

**Key Features**:
- View historical Rotary years with leadership portraits and themes
- See automatically calculated statistics (meetings, speakers, projects, beneficiaries, project value)
- Edit year narratives, highlights, and challenges (officers/chairs only)
- Browse completed service projects with full details
- View speakers who presented during the year
- Mobile-friendly interface for viewing during meetings

## 1. Accessing the Timeline

### Navigation

1. Open the Georgetown Rotary system in your browser
2. Click **"Timeline"** in the top navigation menu
3. The system will default to the current Rotary year

### Mobile Access

The timeline is fully mobile-responsive:
- Portrait photos display properly on small screens
- Statistics wrap to 2 columns on mobile
- All interactive elements are touch-friendly (44px minimum)
- Swipe to scroll through projects and speakers

## 2. Understanding the Timeline View

### Layout Sections

**Header Section**:
- Club information (Georgetown Rotary Club #85956, District 3300)
- Charter date and anniversary
- Year selector dropdown

**Leadership Themes**:
- **RI President**: Rotary International President's portrait, theme logo, and theme name

- Cards displaying completed projects for the year

**Speakers**:
- Cards displaying speakers who presented
- Click any speaker to view full profile

**Edit Button** (Officers/Chairs only):
- Blue pencil icon in top-right of header

**Changing Years**:
1. Click the year dropdown at the top of the page

**Available Years**:
- All years with records in the database

## 3. Viewing Year Statistics

### Statistics Explained

**Meetings**:
- Automatically calculated from speakers marked "Spoken" with scheduled dates
- Each unique meeting date counts as one meeting
- Updates in real-time when speakers are moved to "Spoken" column

**Speakers**:
- Total number of speakers who presented during the year
- Auto-links when speaker is dragged to "Spoken" column with scheduled date
- Only counts speakers with dates within the Rotary year (July 1 - June 30)

**Projects**:
- Completed service projects with completion dates in the Rotary year
- Auto-links when project status changes to "Completed"
- Updates in real-time via database subscriptions

**Beneficiaries**:
- Sum of all beneficiaries from completed projects
- Automatically calculated from project beneficiary counts
- Updates when projects are completed or edited

**Project Value (RM)**:
- Total Malaysian Ringgit value of completed projects
- Sum of all project_value_rm fields
- Formatted with thousands separator (e.g., "15,000.00")

### Progress-Aware Statistics

**Important**: For the current Rotary year, statistics only count:
- Meetings that have already occurred (past scheduled dates)
- Projects already completed (completion dates in the past)

**Why**: Prevents inflating statistics with future scheduled events.

## 4. Editing Year Narratives (Officers/Chairs Only)

### Who Can Edit?

The **Edit** button only appears for users with officer/chair roles:
- President
- President-Elect
- Secretary
- Treasurer
- Board Chair
- Committee Chairs (with appropriate permissions)

### Opening the Narrative Editor

1. Navigate to the year you want to edit
2. Click the **blue pencil icon** (Edit) in the top-right of the header section
3. The narrative editor modal opens

### Narrative Editor Sections

**1. Year Summary**
- **Purpose**: Brief 2-3 sentence overview of the year
- **Displays**: At the top of the timeline view
- **Best Practice**: Focus on the year's theme, major accomplishments, or defining characteristics
- **Example**: "2025-2026 was a year of growth and innovation for Georgetown Rotary. We completed our largest service project to date, welcomed 8 new members, and strengthened our partnerships with local NGOs."

**2. Detailed Narrative**
- **Purpose**: Full story of the Rotary year
- **Content**: Major initiatives, themes, memorable moments, challenges faced, lessons learned
- **Best Practice**: Write for future officers and members who want to understand the year's context
- **Example**: "Under President Jane Smith's leadership, Georgetown Rotary embraced digital transformation while maintaining our commitment to hands-on service..."

**3. Highlights**
- **Structure**: Title + Description pairs
- **Purpose**: Document major achievements and milestones
- **How to Add**:
  1. Click **"+ Add Highlight"**
  2. Enter a title (e.g., "Record Fundraising Year")
  3. Enter a description (e.g., "Raised RM 75,000 through innovative virtual events, surpassing our goal by 25%")
  4. Click **"+ Add Highlight"** again for more
- **How to Remove**: Click "Remove" on any highlight

**4. Challenges & Resolutions**
- **Structure**: Issue + Resolution pairs
- **Purpose**: Document lessons learned for future leaders
- **How to Add**:
  1. Click **"+ Add Challenge"**
  2. Enter the issue/challenge
  3. Enter how it was resolved or what was learned
  4. Click **"+ Add Challenge"** again for more
- **How to Remove**: Click "Remove" on any challenge

### Auto-Save Feature

**How It Works**:
- Changes are automatically saved **every 2 seconds** after you stop typing
- Save status indicator shows: "✓ Saved", "Saving...", or "✗ Error saving"
- You can close the modal anytime - your work is preserved

**Best Practices**:
- Write naturally - the system saves automatically
- Don't worry about losing work if you close the modal
- If you see "Error saving", check your internet connection and try again

### Closing the Editor

1. Click the **"Done"** button at the bottom
2. Or click the **X** in the top-right corner
3. All changes are saved automatically - no need to click "Save"

### Professional Writing Tips

**Tone**: Professional yet warm - suitable for district presentations and club archives

**Audience**: Future officers, incoming members, district leadership

**Content**:
- Focus on impact and outcomes, not just activities
- Include specific numbers and results
- Acknowledge member contributions
- Document what worked and what didn't (in Challenges section)

**Avoid**:
- Overly casual language
- Unexplained acronyms or jargon
- Negative comments about individuals
- Unverified statistics

## 5. Managing Service Projects

### How Projects Link to Timeline

**Automatic Linking**:
- When a project's status is changed to **"Completed"**, it automatically links to the appropriate Rotary year
- Link is based on the **completion date** field
- Statistics update in real-time

### Setting Completion Date

1. Go to **Service Projects** section
2. Click on a project to open the modal
3. Change status to **"Completed"**
4. Set the **Completion Date** field
5. Click **"Save Changes"**
6. Project now appears on the timeline for that Rotary year

### Best Practices

**Completion Date**:
- Set the date when the project actually finished (not when status was updated)
- Use the final day of the project activity
- Ensure date falls within the correct Rotary year (July 1 - June 30)

**Project Value**:
- Include all costs: materials, volunteer time, partner contributions
- Use total project value, not just club's contribution
- Enter in Malaysian Ringgit (RM)

**Beneficiaries**:
- Count direct beneficiaries only (not indirect impact)
- Use conservative estimates if exact count unknown
- Document counting methodology in project notes

**Timeline Fields** (when status = "Completed"):
- **Completion Date**: Required for auto-linking
- **Lessons Learned**: Optional but valuable for future projects
- **Would Repeat**: Select "Yes", "Yes - With modifications", or "No"
- **Repeat Recommendations**: If applicable, document what to change

## 6. Managing Speakers

### How Speakers Link to Timeline

**Automatic Linking**:
- When a speaker is dragged to **"Spoken"** column in the kanban board, they automatically link to the appropriate Rotary year
- Link is based on the **scheduled date** field
- Meeting count and speaker statistics update in real-time

### Setting Scheduled Date

1. Go to **Speakers** kanban board
2. Click **Edit** on a speaker card
3. Set the **Scheduled Date** to the meeting date
4. Drag speaker to **"Spoken"** column
5. Speaker now appears on the timeline for that Rotary year

### Best Practices

**Scheduled Date**:
- Set to the actual meeting date when speaker presented
- Ensure date falls within the correct Rotary year
- One speaker per meeting (for accurate meeting count)

**Speaker Information**:
- Complete topic and description fields for better timeline display
- Add organization name for context
- Include LinkedIn and website URLs for future reference

**Rotarian Badge**:
- Check "Is Rotarian" if speaker is a Rotarian
- Add their club name in "Rotary Club" field
- Rotarian badge displays on timeline speaker cards

## 7. Monthly Maintenance Tasks

### First Week of July (New Rotary Year)

**Create New Year Record** (CEO/Database Admin):
1. Create new rotary_years record in database
2. Upload Club President portrait photo
3. Upload RI President theme logo
4. Upload District Governor portrait photo (if available)
5. Enter leadership names and themes
6. Set stats to zero (will auto-calculate)

**System Verification**:
1. Navigate to timeline and select new year
2. Verify leadership portraits display correctly
3. Check that statistics start at zero
4. Test narrative editor (add placeholder summary)

### Monthly Reviews

**Week 1 of Each Month**:
1. Review previous month's speakers
   - Verify all moved to "Spoken" column
   - Check scheduled dates are accurate
   - Update speaker information if needed

2. Review completed projects
   - Ensure completion dates are accurate
   - Verify beneficiary counts and project values
   - Add lessons learned while still fresh

3. Check timeline statistics
   - Compare to club records
   - Report any discrepancies
   - Update if corrections needed

**End of Rotary Year (June)**:
1. Complete all year narratives (summary, detailed narrative, highlights, challenges)
2. Verify all speakers marked "Spoken"
3. Ensure all completed projects have completion dates
4. Review statistics for accuracy
5. Export/screenshot timeline for annual report

### Leadership Photo Updates

**When to Update**:
- New Club President (July 1 each year)
- New District Governor (July 1 each year)
- New RI President (July 1 each year)

**How to Upload** (CEO/Database Admin):
1. Upload photo to Supabase Storage bucket:
   - `rotary-portraits` for Club President and DG photos
   - `rotary-themes` for theme logos
2. Update database record with new URL
3. Verify photo displays correctly on timeline

**Photo Requirements**:
- Format: JPEG or PNG
- Size: Max 500KB (optimize before upload)
- Dimensions: 800x800px recommended (square aspect ratio)
- Quality: Professional headshot, good lighting, plain background

## 8. Troubleshooting

### Timeline Not Loading

**Symptoms**: Blank page or loading spinner

**Solutions**:
1. Refresh the page (Cmd+R on Mac, Ctrl+R on Windows)
2. Check your internet connection
3. Try a different browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox)
4. Clear browser cache and reload
5. Contact system administrator if problem persists

### Statistics Not Updating

**Symptoms**: Statistics show zero or outdated numbers

**Solutions**:
1. Verify speakers have scheduled dates set
2. Verify projects have completion dates set
3. Check that dates fall within the correct Rotary year (July 1 - June 30)
4. Refresh the page to reload data
5. Check that auto-linking completed (look for projects/speakers on timeline)

**Manual Verification**:
1. Count speakers marked "Spoken" with dates in the year
2. Count projects marked "Completed" with dates in the year
3. Compare to timeline statistics
4. Report discrepancies to system administrator

### Edit Button Not Visible

**Symptoms**: Can't see Edit button on timeline

**Solutions**:
1. Check your user role - only officers/chairs can edit
2. Ensure you're logged in (if authentication is enabled)
3. Try refreshing the page
4. Contact system administrator to verify your role permissions

### Auto-Save Not Working

**Symptoms**: Save status shows "Saving..." forever or "Error saving"

**Solutions**:
1. Check your internet connection
2. Wait 5 seconds and check if status changes to "Saved"
3. Copy your text to clipboard (just in case)
4. Close and reopen the editor - previous auto-saves are preserved
5. If error persists, try a different browser

### Photos Not Displaying

**Symptoms**: Missing portraits or theme logos

**Solutions**:
1. Wait a few seconds for images to load
2. Check your internet connection
3. Verify photos are uploaded to Supabase Storage
4. Verify database URLs are correct
5. Try viewing in a different browser
6. Contact system administrator if photos still missing

### Mobile Display Issues

**Symptoms**: Layout broken or text too small on phone

**Solutions**:
1. Try rotating phone to landscape orientation
2. Zoom out if needed (pinch gesture)
3. Ensure browser is up to date
4. Try a different mobile browser
5. Report specific issues to system administrator
